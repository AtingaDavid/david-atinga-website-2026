Experience Section
7 Layout Options
Design Preview · David Atinga · davidatinga.netlify.app

Every option below uses your real data. Compare them side by side and pick the one that fits — or mix ideas across options.

01
Full "Show Details" Toggle
▸ Per-role toggle Minimal change Matches your Abstract pattern
All bullet points are hidden by default. Each role shows a "▸ Responsibilities" button that expands the full list. Great for quick scanning — the timeline stays clean until the reader decides to dig in.
02
Headline Bullet + Collapse Rest
▸ Per-role expand Best balance of detail vs. scan
The first (most impactful) bullet is always visible. Remaining bullets are hidden behind a "▸ +N more" toggle. You never lose the hook — the reader sees value immediately and can choose to read more.
03
Expandable Card Grid
Click to expand Big visual change Most scannable
Roles become compact cards in a 2-column grid — no bullets visible by default, just period, role, org, and tags. Clicking a card expands it in place to reveal the full detail. The "+" icon signals interactivity.
04
Split Pane — Master / Detail
Click to load detail Most professional Like an email inbox
A compact list of roles sits on the left; clicking one populates the full detail panel on the right. Both panes are visible simultaneously — very efficient for browsing a dense track record without losing context.
05
Tag-Elevated Summary Layer
No interaction needed Zero JS change Visual reweight only
No toggles or JS — just a visual reordering. Tags move above the bullets and become larger, acting as a scannable "keyword summary" for each role. Bullets are de-emphasised (lighter, smaller) below them. The eye scans tags first, reads bullets if interested.
06
Inline Keyword Highlights
✦ New No interaction needed Full content preserved
All bullets remain fully visible — nothing is hidden. Instead, key terms are highlighted inline: numbers/scale in amber, methodology terms in green, strong action verbs in forest. The highlights become scanning anchors, so a quick pass picks up the key facts without reading every word. A newspaper-style approach.
07
Interactive Tag Filter
✦ New Filter by skill/client Biggest UX upgrade
All roles across all tabs are shown together in a compact flat list. Clicking any tag (e.g. "USAID", "Field Supervision", "Impact Evaluation") instantly filters to show only matching roles — others fade away. Clicking a row expands it to reveal bullet details. Ideal for recruiters looking for specific experience types.
